Cut, don't build

I sat down to expand an app and ended up deleting most of it.

The app started its conception as a tool to help me navigate health metrics and a few experiments around diet and training. All in all the first version was a small thing I built to track my body. Digestion, symptoms, mood. Useful and something I have wanted for a long time. But as I started to plan out the other functions the ambition grew too: a library of protocols, a daily practice generator, training logs. None of it was finished. Some were already in other tools. All of it was placeholder. And every time I described what it should become, it got bigger. By today I was sketching a full desktop dashboard. A personal operating system. Feeds, widgets, everything in one screen.

Somewhere in the middle of that I caught myself. The growing wasn't progress. It was hiding. As long as the thing stayed unfinished and grand, I never had to ship it or use it. A big plan is a comfortable place to not start.

So I cut. I deleted the protocol library, the practice tracking, the training logs. I kept the part that was actually useful (the body and digestion metrics) and added one small thing I'd wanted: a way to log my mental weather, from blue sky to thunderstorm. Two screens. That's it. Now it's done and I use it.

The bigger dashboard idea isn't dead. I wrote it down and parked it. When I come back to it, it'll be because I chose to build it, not because I was avoiding something smaller and more honest.

Cutting felt like more progress than building. I want to remember that.
