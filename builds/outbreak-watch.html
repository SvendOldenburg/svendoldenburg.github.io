Outbreak Watch

A real-time disease outbreak intelligence dashboard. Pulls live signals from WHO Disease Outbreak News and HealthMap, maps them to a world choropleth, tracks trends over time, and surfaces who is reporting and how much to trust them.

Why it exists

The news cycle in May 2026 was a mess of outbreak fragments -- Ebola Bundibugyo declared a PHEIC, a hantavirus cluster on a cruise ship, H5N1 zoonotic spillovers, mpox recombinants, dengue grade 3 in Southeast Asia. All real. All reported in isolation. No coherent picture of what was actually happening or how it was moving.

This is the tool I wanted: a single view that lifts outbreak data out of the news swamp and shows the shape of it. What's active, where, how fast, who says so, and how much you should trust them.

What it shows

  • World map with country choropleth colored by outbreak severity (watch / concern / alert / emergency)
  • Click any country to see all active outbreaks there -- disease, status, case counts, signal growth
  • Live signal feed from WHO DON and HealthMap, filterable by source, disease, and country
  • Trends chart: daily signal volume over 14-90 days, by disease, country, or source
  • Source trust panel: every data source rated on editorial independence and transparency, with funding disclosure

How it works

Two Python scrapers run on the VPS on systemd timers. WHO DON runs every 6 hours, pulling from the WHO OData JSON API (the RSS feed is dead). HealthMap runs every 2 hours, pulling from their alerts endpoint. Both scrapers deduplicate by SHA-256 hash and write to PocketBase.

A third script, the aggregator, runs hourly and rolls raw signals into outbreak events -- grouping by disease and country, upsetting status and case counts. The frontend reads all three layers via PocketBase's public REST API and renders without any server-side code.

The map is Leaflet with Natural Earth GeoJSON polygons. The trends chart is Chart.js. Both are plain vanilla JS with no framework. The stack is identical to the training tracker and Energy -- nginx serving static files behind Traefik on the same VPS.

What's next

Phase 1 is running. Phase 2 will add CDC Socrata and ECDC weekly CSV feeds for richer case count data, better NLP-based event aggregation to reduce manual curation, and alert notifications on new PHEIC declarations.
