# LinkedIn Post Content Ideas

*Last updated: 2026-05-01*

The section is called "Digital Business + AI" -- that's the frame. But the voice is personal and real, not professional-polished. One post per week. Playground for now.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. The Departure
The story of leaving the 9-5. You've opened it -- now you need to deliver what the first post promised ("that's what the next few posts are for"). This is a natural series. Run it until it's done.

### 2. Building with AI
What you're actually learning from building with AI. Not hype, not tutorials -- honest field notes from someone using it daily. The AI Clarity Audit, Claude Code, the dashboards, the solo founder workflow.

### 3. The Solo Operating System
What running everything alone actually looks like. No team, no office, no safety net. Routines, tools, mental models, what breaks, what works. The digital nomad + bootstrapper angle.

### 4. Honest Reckoning
The inner work -- but told through practical, relatable doors. Not "I had a spiritual awakening." More: "I ignored my body for four years and it sent me to the hospital." The self-abandonment story. The gap between who you were at work and who you actually are.

### 5. Contrarian Takes
Short, punchy, one-idea posts. Observations that cut against the grain. Things you've unlearned. Surprises.

**Expanded ideas for the Departure series:**

- **"The blank page nobody warns you about"** -- The moment the script runs out. No alarm, no commute, no meeting at 9. The strange vertigo of total openness. Not "freedom" in the Instagram sense -- the actual disorientation of it. What happens when every possibility is technically open and that somehow makes choosing harder, not easier.

- **"The panic hits at 2pm on a Tuesday"** -- The fear post. Not abstract fear -- specific, bodily, unexpected. The moment it stops feeling like an adventure and starts feeling like a mistake. What that's actually like to sit with. Most relatable for people thinking about leaving but scared to admit the fear is real.

- **"I didn't know what I was looking for. That was the whole point."** -- The curiosity post. The difference between exploring with direction and exploring with genuine openness. The counterintuitive idea that not knowing what you're building toward can be a feature, not a bug -- if you can hold it right.

- **"I can't unknow this now."** -- After 14 months of living differently, you understand the trade. You see what you gain and what you give up on both sides. Frame: not "I could never go back" -- but "I understand the cost of each choice now in a way I didn't before. That clarity is the thing I'd never trade." Self-awareness, not a red flag. The trepidation post, handled carefully.

## Notes on Format and Voice
- Lead with a strong hook -- first line does the work
- Short paragraphs. White space.
- End with a real question or a direct observation, not a CTA
- Don't polish out the uncertainty -- it's the most credible thing you have right now
- The "Digital Business + AI" label doesn't mean every post is about AI. It's the lens. The personal story is the substance.

---

## Scheduling Logic
Don't over-plan the order. The Departure series should run as a thread -- post 2, 3, 4, 5 are already owed. Interleave contrarian takes or solo OS posts when the departure series needs a break.

The brainstorm lives here. When you sit down to write, pick one, run `/linkedin-post`, and go.
