The most stuck I have ever felt was when I had no obligations at all.
Too much freedom is its own kind of prison. It took a few inner crashes on a beach in Da Nang to teach me that.

I had been moving every two or three weeks since I left. New city, new Airbnb, new everything. It felt like a free life. It was also exhausting in a way I hadn't named yet.

In January I stopped. I rented a place in Da Nang for two months and just stayed.

The first thing that happened was nothing. I didn't know what to do with myself.

The second thing was that I started doing things I thought I should be doing. That ended badly.

Then I started doing what I actually wanted to do.

I fell into the practical side of AI. I built things nobody asked for. Tools. Dashboards. Experiments. Some of it was bad. I was okay with that.

There is something that happens when you stop performing productivity and start playing with a thing just to see what it does. Days feel different. Time moves differently.

And this kept coming back to me. How many people never get to feel this? Not because they lack freedom, but because everyday life is so good at filling every gap before you wonder what you would actually put there.

The trap isn't the job. The trap is the rhythm.

I am still figuring out what mine looks like. But I know the difference now.

What would you spend your time on if no one was watching and no reward was waiting?
