April 18, 2026 READY

August 2025. I was supposed to return to the office. I didn't.

August 2025. I was supposed to walk back into the office after six months away.
I didn't.

The sabbatical was self-financed. The team I'd been part of had disbanded while I was gone. And somewhere on the other side of the world, a quiet decision had already happened in me, before I admitted it to anyone, including myself.

No job lined up. No plan B. No clients waiting.

Just enough runway to find out what that actually meant.

In the weeks before, friends kept asking the same thing.

"But what are you going to do?"

I didn't have an answer. That was the whole point.

What followed surprised me in ways I didn't see coming.

The version of me writing this is not the person who cleared that desk.

A lot happened. Some of it I chose. Most of it chose me.

I haven't posted like this anywhere before. But now that I'm back, I want to take you through it. The next few posts are that journey. In English this time, because more of you are reading now.

If you've ever stood at that edge, where the old chapter is clear but the new one is just a feeling, you know what I mean.

Has anyone else made this jump? What was the moment you knew you weren't going back?
