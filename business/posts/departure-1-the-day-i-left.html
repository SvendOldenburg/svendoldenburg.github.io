April 18, 2026 READY

August 2025. I was supposed to return to the office. I didn't.

August 2025. I was supposed to return to the office after a six month self-financed sabbatical. I wasn't ready, and the old team I was part of had disbanded anyway. So I didn't return.

I didn't have another job lined up.

No plan B. No clients waiting. No business already running.

Just a quiet decision that it was time to go, and enough runway to find out what that actually meant.

In the weeks before I left, a few close friends asked me the same thing: "But what are you going to do?"

I didn't have an answer. I knew it didn't make sense. That was the point.

What followed surprised me. In ways I didn't see coming. It's been a wild year.

I don't think I have ever posted like this here (or on any other platform for that matter), but now that I'm back I want to take you with me on a journey. That's what the next few posts are for. And it's going to be in English as I now have a broader circle of connections.

But I'll say this: The version of me writing this is not the same person who cleared that desk.

A lot happened. Some of it I chose. Most of it chose me.

If you've ever stood at that edge, where you can see the old chapter clearly but the new one is still just a feeling, you know what I mean.

The thing I keep coming back to: You don't need to know what you're walking into, when you know it's time for a change, it will be scary no matter what!

Has anyone else made a jump like this? What made you pull the trigger?
