I spent months trying to figure out what to do with my life

I spent months trying to figure out what to do with my life.
Then I stopped. And the days got easy.

Not a breakthrough. Not a plan. Just a shift in how time and energy moved.

In Da Nang I stopped traveling and started paying attention to what I actually did with my hours.

What kept showing up wasn't what I expected.

I fell into building with AI, as play, not strategy. Tools nobody asked for. I was bad at most of it and didn't care.

I started writing. Not reports. Not proposals. Just what I noticed, thought, felt. In English this time, which has its own slow texture.

And underneath both, something harder to put into a LinkedIn sentence. A shift in what I understood to be real. Something that has been cracking open in me for years.

The writing turned out to mostly be about that.

The inner life. What wakes you up. What you find on the other side of the life you thought you were supposed to be living.

I am still learning to describe it. It chose me as clearly as the rest.

What showed up for you when you stopped forcing it?
