I walked around Kastellet on my first day back in Copenhagen and realized I didn't know what home meant anymore

I walked around Kastellet on my first day back in Copenhagen and realized I didn't know what home meant anymore.
Not a bad feeling. Just a new one.

Six months and change away. Roaming Southeast Asia like I was in my early twenties, but with a better budget.

Then a quiet late April morning in Copenhagen, and the rampart path I have walked a hundred times.

I'd expected the relief of being back. I felt it. I had missed the city and the people in it.

Underneath was something harder to name. The city was the same. I wasn't.

There is a word for the space I'm in. Liminal. The threshold between the old chapter and the new one. Not arrival. Not departure. The place in between.

I thought about what this trip would have looked like in my twenties. Different person. Different reasons. I am glad I did it now. The discomfort had a purpose it couldn't have had earlier. The questions it surfaced were ones I had to earn.

I'm sleeping on a guest bed. Signing a lease soon. Building and learning in every direction at once.

What clarified on that walk was simple. If something new needs to happen, it rarely arrives all at once. It comes through small steps in the direction that keeps pulling at you. Slow and steady. Without losing yourself.

What does home mean to you right now?
