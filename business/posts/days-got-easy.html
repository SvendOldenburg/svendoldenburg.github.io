I spent months trying to figure out what to do with my life

I spent months trying to figure out what to do with my life.
Then I stopped. And the days got easy.

Not a breakthrough. Not a plan. Just a shift in how time and energy moved.

In Da Nang, I stopped moving between cities and started paying attention to what I actually did with my hours. And something changed.

I fell into building with AI. Not as a career pivot, not as a strategy. As play. I built things nobody asked for. I was genuinely bad at most of it. I didn't care. There's something clarifying about being a beginner at something that has no audience yet. And Claude Code is a great teacher.

Around the same time, I started writing. Not reports. Not proposals. Not updates for a manager or slides for a board. Just things I noticed, thought, felt. For me and whoever wanted to read.

That freedom was new. I don't think I have ever written like that before. As you can tell, I also started writing in English. That is a refreshing challenge in itself.

And underneath both -- underneath the building and the writing -- there was something else. A shift in what I understood to be real. Like waking up from a life-long sleep in ways I didn't have language for. Lightning struck my soul 4+ years ago, and the last few years cracked that open. Slowly, but unrelenting.

The writing turned out to mostly be about that. The inner life. What wakes you up. What you find on the other side of the life you thought you were supposed to be living.

I'm still learning to describe it. But it chose me as clearly as the rest.

What showed up for you when you stopped forcing it?
