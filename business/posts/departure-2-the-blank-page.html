April 24, 2026 READY

Two weeks out of my job, sitting at a cafe in Bangkok

Three months out of my job, two weeks into Southeast Asia.
I was sitting at a cafe in Bangkok with no idea what to do with my freedom.

Not lost. The opposite. Everything was open at once.

No meeting to prep for. No manager to update. No appointment in the calendar.

Just me, a coffee, and the quiet weight of: what now.

I'd expected relief. I got it, for about a week.

Then the blank page showed up.

It wasn't emptiness. It was too many directions, all of them valid, none of them obvious.

Move somewhere cheaper. Start something. Learn something. Rest. Travel. Do nothing. Do everything.

Nobody warns you about this part. They talk about the courage to leave.

They don't talk about the weeks after, when the adrenaline wears off and you realize no one is coming to hand you the next chapter.

Several months later, I'm still inside it. Still choosing without a script.

Did you ever have a moment where total freedom felt heavier than you expected?
