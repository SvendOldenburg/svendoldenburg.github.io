May 08, 2026 READY

New Year's Day. Muddy rice fields in Hoi An, Vietnam.

New Year's Day, 2026. A muddy rice field in Hoi An, Vietnam.
I sat in a small local cafe wondering what the hell I was doing with my life.

I wasn't regretting anything. But I was terrified.

I'd rented a room with a local family on the edge of the city. Rice fields on every side, flat and endless. The seedlings had just gone in, so the fields were brown and muddy. Beautiful in a few weeks. Not yet.

New Year's carries a specific weight. The year that passed. The one coming.

I walked into those fields with all of it pressing down on me.

The fear hit harder than I expected. Not regret. Just the weight of not knowing how this plays out.

How long can I hold uncertainty before it starts holding me? What if I'm making decisions out of fear?

The fear stayed for days.

What pulled me out wasn't a plan or a timeline. It was a smaller thing I keep relearning.

The mind likes a thinking trap. But the paralysis it produces is made up. I could just move to the next decision. The next action. Stay responsible for the life I was choosing.

That had been enough before. It was enough now.

Have you had a version of that morning? The fear that isn't about regret, just the weight of not knowing?
