May 1, 2026 READY

Four months into Southeast Asia, a stranger from Australia asked what I do

A stranger from Australia asked me what I do, while we waited for our takeaway in some city in Southeast Asia.
I opened my mouth. Nothing coherent came out.

Not embarrassed. I just genuinely didn't know anymore.

For years the answer was automatic. A title, a sector, the elevator speech I used at conferences. A sentence that doubled as an identity.

Somewhere between Vietnam and Bangkok, that sentence stopped being true.

I hadn't even noticed it leave.

The strange part wasn't the void. It was realizing how long I'd been confusing the container for the content.

I always assumed I'd know what I wanted if I just had the time. Then the calendar cleared, really cleared, and it turned out most of what I thought I wanted didn't actually interest me.

That was useful, and also a little bit horrifying.

So I started paying attention differently. Not asking what I should be building, but noticing what I kept returning to without being told. What kept me up. What made an afternoon disappear.

AI. Writing. Spirituality. Understanding why people and systems break down, and what it takes to fix them.

Not from a planning session. From having enough quiet to hear it.

What would you hear, if you turned the volume down?
